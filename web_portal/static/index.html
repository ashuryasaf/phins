Understanding Severe Disability & ADLs

Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) are the fundamental self-care tasks that define functional independence:

  • 1. Bathing: Washing oneself and maintaining personal hygiene
  • 2. Dressing: Putting on and removing clothing independently
  • 3. Eating: Feeding oneself (not meal preparation)
  • 4. Toileting: Using the toilet and maintaining continence
  • 5. Transferring: Moving in/out of bed, chair, wheelchair
  • 6. Continence: Controlling bladder and bowel functions

PHINS Coverage Trigger: Benefits activate when you are permanently unable to perform 3 or more ADLs without substantial assistance. This represents severe functional impairment requiring comprehensive long-term care - the exact gap most insurance policies fail to cover adequately.
